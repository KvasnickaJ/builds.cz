Professional life

After school, I went to Bosch as a Preventive maintenance planner. The position also included general overhaul projects planning and budgeting but mainly, as the best part of the job, machine acceptance and pre-acceptance. I was 1 of 4 people responsible to check machine compliance with Bosch standards and Czech laws from the maintenance and safety of maintenance personnel safety view. The best part was traveling abroad and being next to cutting-edge technology manufacturing machines. I retain my excitement for manufacturing machinery and created a few of them in Fusion360. Recently I was able to find a sponsor for one and build a prototype.

A year came by and my manager noticed several Excel automatization improvements I created in VBA to make my and my colleague's life easier. So I got the opportunity to prove myself with Spindle machine analysis as my first data analyst project. 2 years after and I am a full-blown BI analyst. Meetings to define and polish KPI, creating SQL queries to emulate SAP transactions directly from backend tables, building pipelines in Python, managing MS SQL cache warehouse and creating dashboards in PowerBI.

There were also a lot of opportunities to satisfy curiosity for new technology as part of a working group for Smart Glasses, Maintenance reporting and Predictive maintenance of Staubli robots. And the reason to leave all of this? Some once said people don't leave companies they leave managers. In my case, lack one of the best managers I could have after a forced move.

After Bosch, I start working for Invicti Security as part of the newly emerging data analytics department where I could fully use my talent for data and systems. At the beginning do analysis in Excel to provide first overviews of features for product managers to support their roadmap decisions. Now analysis in tableau for Marketing and Product managers and helping to create a Self-service data platform in Snowflake.

In the future I would like to utilize my talent for programming more, so we will see where the next path will lead.
